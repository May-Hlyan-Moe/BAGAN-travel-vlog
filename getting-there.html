Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Bagan is during the dry season, which typically runs from November to February. During this time, the weather is cooler and more comfortable for exploring the ancient temples and pagodas. Additionally, the skies are clearer, offering stunning sunrise and sunset views over the archaeological site. However, keep in mind that this is also the peak tourist season, so expect larger crowds and higher prices for accommodations. If you prefer to avoid the crowds, consider visiting during the shoulder seasons of October and March when the weather is still pleasant, but tourist numbers are slightly lower.

Gettig There

Whether arriving by air, land, or river, each approach to this archaeological wonderland offers its own tapestry of sights and sensations. Flying into Nyaung U Airport, travelers are greeted by the sight of countless ancient stupas peeking through the lush greenery, signaling the beginning of a journey through history. Those opting for a road trip witness the landscape transform from bustling cityscapes to rural tranquility as they traverse Myanmar's diverse terrain. For the intrepid souls navigating the Ayeyarwady River, the slow, meandering journey provides a glimpse into the rhythms of local life, unfolding a narrative of the country's past and present with each passing village.

Accommodations & Hotels

Accommodation in Bagan ranges from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. Visitors can choose to stay in traditional Burmese-style bungalows or modern hotels with amenities. Many accommodations offer stunning views of the ancient temples, providing a unique and immersive experience. Whether you prefer to stay near the archaeological zone or in the surrounding countryside, there are plenty of options to suit every traveler's taste and budget in Bagan.

Outfit Idea

When exploring Bagan, consider lightweight and breathable clothing suitable for hot weather, such as linen or cotton pants, shorts, or skirts paired with loose-fitting tops or shirts. Don't forget a wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses, and comfortable walking shoes or sandals for exploring the temples. Additionally, carry a lightweight scarf or shawl to cover your shoulders when visiting religious sites.

Transportation in Bagan

Transportation in Bagan primarily revolves around three main options: bicycles, electric scooters, and horse carts. These modes of transport offer varying degrees of flexibility and immersion into the ancient landscape. Bicycles provide a leisurely way to explore the temples at one's own pace, allowing for spontaneous stops and detours along the way. Electric scooters offer a convenient and eco-friendly alternative, particularly for covering greater distances or navigating rough terrain. For a more traditional experience, horse carts offer a charming mode of transportation, evoking a sense of nostalgia as they clip-clop along the dusty paths, transporting visitors back in time amidst the timeless splendor of Bagan's archaeological treasures. Additionally, taxis and private car hires are available for those seeking more comfort and convenience, particularly for longer journeys or exploring areas beyond the temple plains.
